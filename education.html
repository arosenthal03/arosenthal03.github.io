Program & Courses

Program
Business Technology Management
Learn how to analyze business needs, design appropriate technology-based processes and solutions, and communicate these effectively. Also learn to lead work-based teams, participate effectively in projects and understand best practices of organizational change.
Courses

Year 1

Semester 1
CMN279 - Introduction to Professional Communication
Introduces students to contemporary strategies of successful communication in professional contexts.
GMS200 - Introduction to Global Management
Introduces the concepts and complexities of the contemporary business environment with an emphasis on competitiveness, quality, and the main functional areas of management: Planning, Organizing, Controlling, and Leadership.
ITM100 - Foundations of Information Systems
Designed to introduce students to contemporary information systems and demonstrate how these systems are used throughout global organizations.
ITM107 - Managerial Decision MakingEmphasizes how to apply various mathematical techniques in the support of managerial decisions in the various functional areas of business.
SSH105 - Critical ThinkingExamine the structures and principles that undergird all forms of rational discourse, and we will learn how to assess the logical strength and persuasiveness of particular discourses.
Semester 2
ECN104 - Introductory Microeconomics
Concerned with basic concepts of demand and supply, the theory of the firm, and distribution of income.
ITM207 - Computer-Enabled Problem Solving
Covers the basic data representation and processing constructs necessary to problem solving using computers.
MHR405 - Organizational Behaviour
Introduces students to theories of organizational behaviour.
QMS210 - Applied Statistics for Business
Introduces both descriptive and inferential statistics.
CHY183 (Elective) - Introduction to Forensic Sciences
Will provide non-science students with an introduction to the field of forensic science.

Year 2

Semester 1
ACC100 - Introductory Financial Accounting
Designed to give students a basic understanding of accounting for business decisions.
ITM200 - Fundamentals of Programming
Covers the fundamental principles of object-oriented, event-driven program design and implementation in a business environment.
ITM301 - IT Infrastructure
Provides an introduction to computer hardware/software and communication networks.
MKT100 - Principles of Marketing
Introduces students to the fundamental building blocks of marketing: the theories, principles and concepts of market behaviour; marketing metrics and financial marketing; and the skills to leverage this knowledge with IT tools to execute marketing strategies.
